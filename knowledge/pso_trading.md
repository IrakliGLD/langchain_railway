# PSO Trading and Procurement Structure

## Definition
Public Service Obligation (PSO) entities are electricity market participants subject to tariff regulation by GNERC. PSO activities include electricity transmission, distribution (natural monopolies), and certain generation units assigned PSO status for specific policy reasons (price stability, consumer protection, system stability, or dedicated supply obligations).

For tariff methodology, regulated entity tables, and pricing formulas, see `tariffs.md`.

---

## PSO Suppliers (Procurement Side)

Two main regulated electricity suppliers procure electricity to serve end consumers:

### Telmico (Tbilisi Electricity Supplier Company)
- Serves the Tbilisi region
- Procures electricity for two segments:
  - **Universal Service** — residential and small consumers
  - **Public Service** — other regulated supply obligations

### EP Georgia Supply
- Serves regions outside Tbilisi
- Same two-segment structure: Universal Service and Public Service

## PSO Trading Results (2024–2025)

Based on GNERC Market Monitoring Reports for 2024 and 2025.

### Enguri and Vardnili HPP Cascade

The cascade is the primary PSO generator and the largest state-owned hydropower asset.

| Metric | 2024 | 2025 | Change |
|--------|------|------|--------|
| Total Generation | 4,081 million kWh | — | — |
| Supply to Abkhazia | 46.4% | 43.7% | -2.7 pp |
| Regulated Suppliers | 35.9% | 47.0% | +11.1 pp |
| Exports | 17.2% | 7.3% | -9.9 pp |
| Direct Consumers | 0.6% | 0.9% | +0.3 pp |

**Key observation:** In 2025, a significant shift occurred — the share going to regulated suppliers increased substantially while exports dropped sharply. This directly increased the availability of cheap hydro electricity for the domestic regulated supply chain.

## Key Market Insights

### Telmico's Balancing Reliance
- Telmico's Public Service segment has a very high and increasing dependence on balancing electricity (80% → 88.5%)
- In contrast, Universal Service shifted away from balancing (37% → 25.4%) toward PSO plants and deregulated sources
- This divergence reflects different procurement strategies for the two service segments

### Cascade Distribution Shift
- The Enguri/Vardnili cascade's reduced export share (-9.9 pp) and increased supply to regulated suppliers (+11.1 pp) in 2025 directly increased domestic availability of cheap regulated hydro electricity
- This shift may have contributed to lower balancing prices by reducing the need for more expensive balancing sources

### Deregulated Source Growth
- Both Telmico and EP Georgia Supply increased their share of procurement from deregulated plants for Universal Service in 2025
- This is consistent with the ongoing deregulation timeline (see `tariffs.md` for plant-level deregulation dates)

### Structural Difference Between Suppliers
- EP Georgia Supply relies much more heavily on PSO plants for Universal Service (86%) compared to Telmico (60.3%)
- EP Georgia Supply's Public Service procurement structure remained completely stable between 2024 and 2025
- Telmico shows more dynamic procurement shifts year to year

---

## Analytical Guidelines

- When analyzing balancing electricity demand and price, consider PSO supplier procurement structures — shifts in how Telmico and EP Georgia procure electricity affect balancing segment volumes
- A decrease in balancing electricity procurement by regulated suppliers reduces balancing segment demand, which may affect balancing price formation
- The Enguri/Vardnili cascade distribution is a key variable — changes in export vs. domestic supply allocation directly affect available cheap hydro for the regulated supply chain
- PSO trading data comes from GNERC Market Monitoring Reports and reflects annual outcomes, not monthly dynamics
